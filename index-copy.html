E-commerce Packaging Waste

Take a look of its impact, and ways to solve it.

What is E-Commerce Packaging Waste?

Definition: E-commerce packaging waste refers to the discarded materials used to package and deliver online orders, including cardboard boxes, plastic wrapping, bubble wrap, Styrofoam, and other materials. These materials contribute significantly to global waste streams.

With the rise of online shopping, packaging waste has surged. In 2021, the global e-commerce market reached $4.9 trillion, creating massive amounts of waste from excessive packaging used for delivery.

  • Overuse of non-recyclable materials, lack of sustainable alternatives, and the growing consumer demand for fast, convenient deliveries contribute to the rise in packaging waste.
  • Environmental degradation, including overflowing landfills, increased carbon footprints, ocean pollution, and harm to wildlife. Non-biodegradable packaging can persist in ecosystems for hundreds of years, breaking down into microplastics that affect marine life.

According to a report by the World Economic Forum, e-commerce packaging accounts for 165 billion packages annually, and this number is expected to continue to grow.

According to a report by Oceana, a marine conservation organization, Amazon alone generated 465 million pounds of plastic packaging waste in 2019. This waste includes air pillows, bubble wrap, and other plastic packaging items added to the approximately 7 billion Amazon packages delivered in 2019.

The packaging waste generated by online shopping has a significant impact on the environment.

Plastic packaging waste, in particular, is a major problem, as it takes hundreds of years to break down and often ends up polluting the oceans and harming marine life.

Impacts of E-Commerce Packaging Waste

E-commerce packaging waste leads to increased environmental degradation, contributing to overflowing landfills, ocean pollution, and harm to wildlife. It also adds to the carbon footprint, depletes natural resources, and strains waste management systems.

Increased Landfill Usage

The growing amount of non-recyclable packaging from e-commerce contributes to overflowing landfills, which leads to environmental degradation and takes up valuable land space.

Ocean Pollution

A large portion of plastic packaging waste ends up in oceans, where it harms marine life and ecosystems by breaking down into microplastics, which can be ingested by fish and other sea creatures.

Carbon Footprint

The production, transportation, and disposal of packaging materials contribute to greenhouse gas emissions, exacerbating global warming and climate change.

Resource Depletion

The manufacturing of packaging materials like plastics, cardboard, and styrofoam consumes significant amounts of natural resources, such as petroleum for plastic and trees for paper-based materials.

Harm to Wildlife

Animals can mistake packaging waste for food or become entangled in it, leading to injury or death. Non-biodegradable materials can persist in ecosystems for hundreds of years, causing long-term harm to wildlife.

Waste Management Costs

The increase in packaging waste places a burden on local waste management systems, leading to higher costs for sorting, recycling, or landfilling, which can ultimately be passed on to consumers through taxes or fees.

Call To Action

Use of Recyclable and Biodegradable Materials

Shift to packaging made from materials that can be easily recycled or that biodegrade naturally, reducing the amount of waste sent to landfills.

Implement Reusable Packaging Systems

Encourage the use of reusable packaging options that can be returned and reused multiple times, significantly lowering the demand for single-use materials.

Optimize Packaging Sizes

Design packaging that fits the product perfectly, minimizing excess material use and reducing shipping volume, which also cuts down on carbon emissions.

Encourage Consumer Recycling

Provide clear instructions on how to recycle packaging and offer incentives for customers who participate in sustainable disposal practices.
